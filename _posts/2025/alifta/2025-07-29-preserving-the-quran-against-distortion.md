### Question
A friend of mine raises doubts regarding the authenticity of the Mus-hafs (copies of the Qur’an) we have nowadays. He claims that they contain interpolations and, according to him, there was a scribe who refused to hand the manuscript (copy) of the Qur’an that he had to 'Uthman ibn 'Affan (may Allah be pleased with him), which was in Yemen. My friend went on elaborating his claims and told me about the book from which he derived the information upon which he bases his argument. I became angry and refused to listen to his claims any longer. I am writing to you requesting your Fatwa on this issue and information on how the Qur’an was transmitted. I usually face such situations for being a member in Tabligh and Da‘wah (a group calling to Islam).

### Answer:
The Qur’an is Allah’s Words which Jibril (Gabriel, peace be upon him) received from Allah and recited to Muhammad (peace be upon him), who in turn listened and learned it carefully from Jibril exactly as spoken by Allah (Glorified and Exalted be He). Allah (Exalted be He) caused Muhammad (peace be upon him) to memorize it by heart. Allah (Exalted be He) says: **"Move not your tongue concerning (the Qur’ân, O Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم) to make haste therewith. It is for Us to collect it and to give you (O Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم) the ability to recite it (the Qur’ân). And when We have recited it to you [O Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم through Jibrîl (Gabriel)], then follow its (the Qur’an’s) recital. Then it is for Us (Allâh) to make it clear (to you)."** [(Al-Qiyamah, 75:16-19)](/quran?verse=75:16-19)

In his Tafsir (exegesis of the Qur’an), Ibn Jarir (may Allah be merciful to him) said: **"The exegetes of the Qur’an differed regarding the reason why it was said to the Prophet (peace be upon him): Move not your tongue concerning (the Qur’ân, O Muhammad صلى الله عليه وسلم) to make haste therewith. Some of them view that this was said to him because once any part of the Qur’an was revealed to the Prophet (peace be upon him), he would hasten to memorize it out of deep love for it. Hence, he was ordered not to hasten to recite the revelation in order to memorize it; Allah will engrave it on his memory. Other exegetes view that this was said to the Prophet (peace be upon him) because he was always reciting the Qur’an for fear that he might forget it. Therefore, he was ordered not to move his tongue hastily reciting the Qur’an, for Allah would commit it to his chest, and make him able to recite it in the same way that it was revealed to him. Since it is Allah Who would make him recite the Qur’an, he shall not forget it."** [^1]

Allah (Exalted be He) says: **"Verily, We, it is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Qur’ân) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption)."** [(Al-Hijr, 15:9)](/quran/?verse=15:9) Thus, Allah safeguarded the Qur’an for Muslims. The Qur’an was transmitted from the Prophet (peace be upon him) to his Sahabah (Companions of the Prophet), who committed it to memory and writing and transmitted it to future generations fresh and pure, without any distortion or interpolation in it. It was compiled into a single book by Abu Bakr Al-Siddiq (may Allah be pleased with him) during his caliphate under the supervision of Zayd ibn Thabit (may Allah be pleased with him). Then it was complied by `Uthman during his caliphate according to one Harf (mode of recitation), so that the Ummah (nation based on one creed) would not disagree therein. 

Thus, whoever claims that the Qur’an is not divinely-guarded and preserved or has been exposed to any additions or deletions is astray and is leading others astray. Such a person should be asked to repent. If he does not repent, the authorities must impose capital punishment on him for Riddah (apostasy), because his claim contradicts the Statement of Allah (Glorified and Exalted be He): **"Verily, We, it is We Who have sent down the Dhikr (i.e. the Qur’ân) and surely, We will guard it (from corruption)."** [(Al-Hijr, 15:9)](/quran/?verse=15:9) It also conflicts with the Ijma‘ (unanimous consensus) of the Ummah that the Qur’an is divinely-safeguarded. On this basis, Muslim scholars have denounced the claim of the Batiniyyah Shiites that the Qur’an Muslims have today is flawed and that the version they have is the preserved and perfect one. Indeed, this is one of the worst lies. 

May Allah grant us success. May peace and blessings be upon our Prophet Muhammad, his family, and Companions.
